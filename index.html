Overview

Personalized Visual Intelligence (PVI) focuses on developing computer vision algorithms that deliver intelligent services tailored to individual users, ranging from personal item detection and retrieval to customized 3D object capture. By incorporating user-specific knowledge and preferences, PVI enables a wide range of personalized applications that extend beyond the scope of conventional visual perception systems. For example, when integrated with wearable cameras, PVI systems can detect and track personal belongings, helping users locate misplaced items such as keys and wallets in their daily environments. In multi-user settings, such as households, the system can distinguish among family members and accurately recognize and locate each individual's possessions. PVI can also facilitate the digitization of treasured personal objects through customized 3D scanning simply using a smartphone. More broadly, PVI shifts the focus of computer vision from generic object and scene understanding to the perception, modeling, and interaction of user-specific objects and environments. This paradigm opens new opportunities for personalized intelligent services and represents an important step toward human-centered visual intelligence.

Topics

This workshop aims to bring together computer vision researchers and practitioners from both academia and industry who are interested in the topics below (but not limited to):

  • instance representation methods , such as text description, reference images, implicit representation, feature representation, and explicit modeling.
  • personalized instance perception, including instance detection and tracking, instance reconstruction, instance rendering and editing, etc.
  • customized object 3D capture, including 3D reconstruction, and interactive modeling.
  • benchmarks and datasets for the above tasks.
